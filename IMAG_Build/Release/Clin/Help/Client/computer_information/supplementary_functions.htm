Placing the Workstation:

  • The workstation can be installed on a desk, modular shelving or mobile cart.

  • The PC and monitor should be connected to an electrical surge protection device.

  • The entire system should be physically secured to prevent loss (including prevention of access to internal parts.)

  • The workstation should not be installed near heat sources, magnetic fields or liquids.

  • The PC will require periodic maintenance (i.e. removal of accumulated dust and dirt.)

  • Imaging workstations can also attach to accessories, such as scanners, video cameras and microscopes. The workstation storage area should provide protection and support for these accessories.

 

The imaging support staff should meet with the medical staff who will be using the workstation to determine the best location for the workstation.

  • Many issues should be considered before selecting a location.

  • Issues such as mobility of the workstation and its distance from other equipment.

 

Locating an imaging workstation can be a challenge, especially when a workstation is in an operating room or other location that provides acute care.

 

The basic imaging workstation usually consists of :

  • A PC running the Windows NT or Windows 2000 operating system.

  • The monitor screen should be at least 17 diagonal inches and capable of displaying the resolution required for image display. The minimum resolution should be 1024 x 768 pixels with 16 million colors.  

  • All Windows workstations use a mouse, track ball, or digital pad to allow users to issue commands and navigate through applications.
