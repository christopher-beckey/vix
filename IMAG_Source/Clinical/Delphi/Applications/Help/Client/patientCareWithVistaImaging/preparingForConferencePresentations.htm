Preparing for conference presentations

Introduction:

A major use of VistA Imaging is for patient presentations during conferences.

 

Capture of Images for a Conference

If the images to be shown are not already in the system, they should be captured from their storage media (e.g., microscope slides, videotapes, photographs, films).  Please refer to the instructions for capturing images. The image description field can be used for any notes specific to the image. Be sure to link the images to their corresponding procedure or report. The VistA Imaging developers recommend that written notes are made of the patient identifications and images for the conference.

 

Testing Display Workstation Before Conference

Before the conference, a member of the conference team should check the workstation in the conference room to make sure that everything is working properly. During the test, view the images that are planned to be used during the presentation to make sure that there are no unexpected workstation, projector, server, or VistA problems. Viewing the images will also cause VistA Imaging to move jukebox images to magnetic storage for more rapid display during the presentation. If a problem is encountered during the test, notify the system manager immediately.

 

Creating Slides From Online Images

Recently one of our staff captured a series of teaching slides from a very unusual case into the VistA Imaging System. Our Medical Media Service had 35 mm slides, generated directly from those VistA images ready for him in order to discuss the case at the adjoining university hospital as he left the building that evening.
