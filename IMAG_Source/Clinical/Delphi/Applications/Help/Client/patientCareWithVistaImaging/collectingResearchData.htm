Collecting research data

Introduction:

Medical research is conducted on an ongoing basis at all medical centers. Researchers are often faced with the need to store text as well as images in order to document the stages of their studies. Storing and retrieving large amounts of visual data are tasks well suited to computerization.

 

Collecting Images

Most of the medical equipment used by patient care providers are capable of producing digital images. Those units that are not able to produce a direct digital image usually provide a video display. VistA Imaging equipment can be configured to attach to these medical systems and provide a means to save images in patient files. Other systems can provide these images as well. Some examples of these systems are still and motion video cameras and black and white or color scanners. VistA Imaging allows users to add descriptive text to the images while they are being saved.

 

Organizing Data

VistA Imaging can be used to sort the images collected and arrange a set of image copies in whatever order that may be desired.

 

Presenting Research

VistA Imaging can be used to export images to commercial off-the-shelf software. The file formats of the exported files are in common, standard formats. Commercial software can then be used to add the images to desktop presentation software.
