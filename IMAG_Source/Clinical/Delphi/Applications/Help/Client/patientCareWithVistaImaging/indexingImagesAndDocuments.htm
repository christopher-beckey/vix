Indexing images and documents

In VistA Imaging, "indexing" describes the process used to categorize an image (or document) in such a way that the image can be easily retrieved.

 

Requirement: All document scanning must be at a minimum of 300 x 300 dpi.

 

It is very important that facilities across the VHA use consistent index terms for retrieving scanned documents. This will allow users to find the documents they need when they are looking at a patient’s record across the wide area network. It will allow proper sorting of scanned documents at consolidated sites and in the future clinical data repository.

 

Index terms are assigned to images at the time of capture.  These terms include:

  • Patient’s identification (name, number)

  • Date and time of the study or event

  • Unique ID assigned to the study (such as case number or accession number)

  • Type of document, procedure, event, or progress note

  • Specialty and procedure/event

  • Image description

Some index terms are based on information obtained directly from VistA. Others are assigned by the user based on the characteristics of the document.

When the Clinical Capture is used, index terms are assigned to an image (or scanned document) based on information shown on the left side of the Capture window.
