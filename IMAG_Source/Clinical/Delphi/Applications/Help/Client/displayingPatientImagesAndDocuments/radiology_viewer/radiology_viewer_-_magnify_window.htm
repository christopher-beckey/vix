Radiology Viewer - magnify window

Use the magnify window to zoom in on a small portion of the image.  Click on the ’Magnify’ button on the toolbar.  The button will be depressed, and the small Magnify Window will appear in the upper left corner of the radiology window.

 

Use the scrollbar at the right of the magnify window to increase (down) or decrease (up) the magnification.

 

 

Place the mouse cursor over the area of the image to magnify. Click the mouse down and a portion of the image will appear in the magnify window. It will be magnified.  Move the mouse around on the image while the right button is depressed, and the area in the magnify window will change to show the image region near the cursor. The mouse button can be released before moving the mouse.  Then when the mouse is clicked, the magnify window will show the image region near the cursor.

 

Clicking on the ‘Magnify’ button will close the magnify window. The magnify window will also close when the AutoWinLev button or the Pan button is clicked.

 

If you click on another image, it will be selected.  Clicking again will activate the Magnify function for the new selected window.

To choose between Magnifying box (demonstrated before) and Magnifying glass, click on Options in the menu bar.  Then select Magnify, and click on "Magnifying Glass".  Of course, you may go back to the Magnifying Box option by choosing "Magnifying Box".
